Kinematics is the study of motion in relation to position, velocity and acceleration \cite{Buss2022}. In contrast, dynamics is the study of motion with respect to mass, force and torque. Our specific kinematics problem requires us to study the position and velocity of our models. For a given articulated body system, motion can be achieved through applying rotation or translation to the joints of the system. The main purpose of this motion is to move the end effector of the articulated body to a target position. The setup of our articulated body is \textbf{shown below} with 10 joints including the root. For each trial, the orientation of the articulated body is aligned along the y-axis with joint angles of 0 degrees. Each link has a length of 4 making total reachable area a circle with radius 40 centered on the origin $(0,0)$. The following results were obtained from simulations run on a Ryzen 5 7600X 6-core cpu running unity 6.3. Our code is on github [link].

During testing, we ran 5000 trials per methods for different tolerance levels of $0.1, 0.08$ and $0.06$, accumulating over $60000$ data. For each trial, a randomly generated target within the reachable zone is chosen. Keep in mind, for the Jacobian Damped Least Squares, the damping factor for all trials was $0.1$. Several tests and comparisons have been implemented to compare and contrast all the methods mentioned above.
